Jingtao Li

Jingtao Li is currently a 5th-year PhD student at Arizona State University. He received the B.Eng. degree in Micro-electronic Science and Enginnering from UESTC in 2018 and won the 2018 UESTC outstanding student award with highest honors. Currently, he is working as a research intern at PPML (privacy-preserving machine learning) team in Sony AI, mentored by Dr. Lingjuan Lyu.

At ASU, his research projects focus on efficiency, security and privacy aspects in machine learning, under the supervision of Dr. Chaitali Chakrabarti. And he also works as Research Assistant in hardware-oriented implementation of various applications, in collaboration with Dr. Trevor Mudge. Prior to join ASU, he worked as research intern at KAUST, Saudi Arabia, in the field of semiconductor device design. During his undergraduate, he worked as a research assistant under the supervision of Dr. Hua Fan and Dr. Franco Maloberti in analog-to-digital converter design.

Research

I'm interested in efficiency, security and privacy fields of machine learning systems. Representative papers are highlighted.

ResSFL: A Resistance Transfer Framework for Defending Model Inversion Attack in Split Federated Learning
Jingtao Li, Adnan Siraj Rakin, Xing Chen, Zhezhi He, Deliang Fan, Chaitali Chakrabarti
CVPR, 2022
Paper / Code

This work for the first time, successfully defend model inversion attack in Split Federated Learning with the help of a two-step framework called ResSFL. The proposed ResSFL framework achieves effective attack mitigation (10x error) with high utility (less than 2% accuracy drop). With special design, we reduced the memory and computation overhead to an acceptable level.

NeurObfuscator: A Full-stack Obfuscation Tool to Mitigate Neural Architecture Stealing
Jingtao Li, Zhezhi He, Adnan Siraj Rakin, Deliang Fan, Chaitali Chakrabarti
HOST, 2021
Paper / Code

We present the Neurobfuscator, the first in successfully prevent neural architecture stealing attack on GPU systems. We obfuscate a ResNet-18 ImageNet model to a totally different architecture (with 44 layer difference) to the view of the attacker. The obfuscation is functionality-equivalent and comes with only 2% overall latency overhead.

T-bfa: Targeted bit-flip adversarial weight attack
Adnan Siraj Rakin, Zhezhi He, Jingtao Li, Fan Yao, Chaitali Chakrabarti, Deliang Fan
TPAMI
Paper / Code

We propose the first work of targeted BFA based (T-BFA) adversarial weight attack on DNNs, which can intentionally mislead selected inputs to a target output class.

Communication and Computation Reduction for Split Learning using Asynchronous Training
Xing Chen, Jingtao Li, Chaitali Chakrabarti
IEEE-SIPS
Paper / Code

We proposes a loss-based asynchronous training scheme to effectively reduce the communication overhead of Split Learning by 106.7x at most.

Ra-bnn: Constructing robust & accurate binary neural network to simultaneously defend adversarial bit-flip attack and improve accuracy
Adnan Siraj Rakin, Yang Li, Jingtao Li, Fan Yao, Chaitali Chakrabarti, Yu Cao, Jae-son Seo, Deliang Fan
Arxiv
Paper / Code

We propose RA-BNN that adopts a complete binary neural network to significantly improve DNN model robustness

Radar: Run-time adversarial weight attack detection and accuracy recovery
Jingtao Li, Adnan Siraj Rakin, Zhezhi He, Deliang Fan, Chaitali Chakrabarti
DATE, 2021
Paper / Code

We present RADAR, the first work of detecting bit-flip attack on machine learning systems, with a light-weight fast checking & recovery mechanism.
Defending bit-flip attack through dnn weight reconstruction
Jingtao Li, Adnan Siraj Rakin, Yan Xiong, Liangliang Chang, Zhezhi He, Deliang Fan, Chaitali Chakrabarti
DAC, 2020
Paper / Code

We propose a novel weight reconstruction method that effectively mitigate bit-flips on quantized neural networks, either happen naturally or injected by malicious attackers.
Defending and harnessing the bit-flip based adversarial weight attack
Zhezhi He, Adnan Siraj Rakin, Jingtao Li, Chaitali Chakrabarti, Deliang Fan
CVPR, 2020
Paper / Code

We propose to leverage binarization-aware training and its relaxation - piece-wise clustering as simple and effective countermeasures to BFA.
Order statistics and optimal selection of unit elements in DACs to enhance the static linearity
Hua Fan, Jingtao Li, Franco Maloberti
TCAS-I
Paper / Code

We propose ordering technique to improve the static linearity performances of digital-to-analog converters with in-depth study in grouping/sequencing elements in segmented, binary and unary DAC architectures.
